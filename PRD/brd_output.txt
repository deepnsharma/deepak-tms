Introduction</h1><h2>1.1 Purpose</h2><p>This Business Requirements Document (BRD) defines the functional and integration requirements for the Transporter Portal — a web-based self-service platform that extends SAP Transportation Management System (TMS) capabilities to external transporter partners. The document is intended for the development and implementation team responsible for building the portal.</p><h2>1.2 Background</h2><p>The organization currently manages transportation operations through SAP TMS. Transporters, however, have no direct visibility into delivery orders, contract rates, trip status, or invoice workflows — all coordination is done manually. This portal aims to eliminate that gap by providing transporters a secure, real-time window into relevant data and enabling two-way interaction for key business processes.</p><h2>1.3 Scope</h2><p>The Transporter Portal covers the following functional areas:</p><ul><li>Dashboard with key performance analytics</li><li>Contract and rate line visibility</li><li>Vehicle and Driver master data management</li><li>Delivery Order assignment and vehicle placement</li><li>Proof of Delivery (POD) submission</li><li>Freight invoice creation and approval workflow</li></ul><p>The portal will serve two user types: Transporters (external) and Internal Users (operations/finance team).</p><h2>1.4 Assumptions &amp; Constraints</h2><ul><li>SAP TMS is the system of record. The portal is a front-end interface that reads from and writes back to SAP.</li><li>All integration details (API design, middleware selection, error handling) are to be determined by the implementation team. This document captures what data must flow, not how.</li><li>Field-level design for Vehicle and Driver masters must follow SAP master data structure.</li><li>Approval workflow levels are fixed at three tiers as specified in this document.</li></ul><h1>2. User Roles &amp; Access</h1><p>The portal has two distinct user types with different access levels:</p><table><thead><tr><th><p><strong>Page / Feature</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Transporter</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Internal User</strong></p></th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><p>Dashboard</p></td><td><p>✔ (own data only)</p></td><td><p>✔ (all transporters, with filter)</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Contracts</p></td><td><p>✔ (own contracts)</p></td><td><p>✔ (all transporters, with filter)</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Vehicle &amp; Driver Master</p></td><td><p>✔ (own vehicles/drivers)</p></td><td><p>✔ (all transporters, with filter)</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Place Vehicle</p></td><td><p>✔</p></td><td><p>✗ — Not Applicable</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Create POD</p></td><td><p>✔</p></td><td><p>✗ — Not Applicable</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Submit Invoice</p></td><td><p>✔</p></td><td><p>✗ — Not Applicable</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Review Invoice</p></td><td><p>✗ — Not Applicable</p></td><td><p>✔</p></td></tr></tbody></table><p>Internal users can see data across all transporters. A Transporter filter must be available on every page visible to internal users.</p><h1>3. Dashboard</h1><h2>3.1 Overview</h2><p>The Dashboard is the first page a transporter sees upon login. It provides a snapshot of their operational performance through KPI cards and visual charts. Internal users also see a dashboard, but with visibility across all transporters (with a transporter filter).</p><h2>3.2 Date Filter</h2><p>All dashboard metrics must be filterable by date range. Default view: Current Month. Options to include: Last Month, Custom Range.</p><h2>3.3 KPI Cards (Top Row)</h2><table><thead><tr><th><p><strong>KPI</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Definition</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Format</strong></p></th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><p>Ongoing Trips</p></td><td><p>Trips where the trip has been initiated in SAP but POD is not yet completed</p></td><td><p>Count</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Trips Completed This Month</p></td><td><p>Trips initiated and POD completed within the selected period</p></td><td><p>Count</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Pending Acceptance</p></td><td><p>Delivery Orders assigned to the transporter awaiting vehicle placement</p></td><td><p>Count</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Open Invoices</p></td><td><p>Freight invoices submitted but not yet approved/paid</p></td><td><p>Amount (₹)</p></td></tr></tbody></table><h2>3.4 Visual Charts</h2><table><thead><tr><th><p><strong>Chart</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Type</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Description</strong></p></th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><p>On-Time Vehicle Placement</p></td><td><p>Progress Bar / Gauge</p></td><td><p>Scheduled vehicle reporting date vs. actual reporting date. X-axis: quantity; Y-axis: product names.</p></td></tr></tbody></table><p><em>🔗 Integration: All KPI and chart data is sourced from SAP TMS and SAP Finance modules. The portal must pull this data via real-time or near-real-time integration. Data points include trip status, invoice status, vehicle placement timestamps, and freight quantities.</em></p><h1>4. Contracts</h1><h2>4.1 Overview</h2><p>The Contracts page displays all active contract lines associated with the logged-in transporter. This is a read-only view. The unit of display is the contract line (not the contract header), as a single transporter may have multiple lines across companies, products, and routes.</p><h2>4.2 Contract Line Fields</h2><table><thead><tr><th><p><strong>#</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Field</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Description</strong></p></th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><p>1</p></td><td><p>Company</p></td><td><p>Group company for which the contract line is applicable. Any updates made to rate or contract tables in SAP TMS must automatically reflect on the portal without manual intervention. No contract data entry is done on the portal side.</em></p><h1>5. Vehicle &amp; Driver Master</h1><h2>5.1 Overview</h2><p>This page contains two tabs — Vehicles and Drivers — and is the only page with bidirectional (two-way) integration between the portal and SAP. Transporters can view existing records (synced from SAP) and also add new records, which are pushed back to SAP master data.</p><h2>5.2 Vehicle Master</h2><h3>Fields</h3><p>Indicative fields include: Vehicle Number, RC Number, Insurance Policy Number, Insurance Expiry Date, Vehicle Capacity, Vehicle Type, Number of Compartments. The full field list must be aligned with the SAP Vehicle Master.</p><p><em>📌 Dev Note: Vehicle master fields on the portal must be built in strict 1:1 alignment with the corresponding Vehicle Master fields in SAP TMS. No additional or custom fields should be introduced without explicit business sign-off.</em></p><p><em>🔗 Integration: Two-way sync: (1) Vehicles already maintained in SAP appear on the portal automatically. (2) When a transporter adds a new vehicle via the portal, the record is created in SAP Vehicle Master. Updates in either system must reflect in the other.</em></p><h2>5.3 Driver Master</h2><h3>Fields</h3><p>Indicative fields include: Driver Name, Aadhaar Card Number, Driving License Number, License Expiry Date. The full field list must be aligned with the SAP Driver Master.</p><p><em>📌 Dev Note: Driver master fields on the portal must be built in strict 1:1 alignment with the corresponding Driver Master fields in SAP TMS. No additional or custom fields should be introduced without explicit business sign-off.</em></p><p><em>🔗 Integration: Two-way sync: Same as Vehicle Master. Drivers in SAP appear on the portal, and new drivers added via portal are created in SAP Driver Master.</em></p><h1>6. Place Vehicle</h1><h2>6.1 Overview</h2><p>The Place Vehicle page is where transporters accept and respond to Delivery Orders (DOs) issued by the business. This page drives the core load assignment workflow.</p><h2>6.2 DO Assignment Logic</h2><p>The following sequence governs how a Delivery Order reaches a transporter:</p><ul><li>A Delivery Order is created in SAP TMS.</li><li>The system checks the active contract/rate table to identify which transporter has the lowest rate for the Origin-Destination-Product combination specified in the DO.</li><li>The DO is pushed to that transporter's Place Vehicle page.</li><li>If two or more transporters share the lowest rate for the same lane, the DO is assigned randomly among them.</li></ul><h2>6.3 Acceptance Window &amp; Cascading Logic</h2><p>Each DO is visible to the assigned transporter for a configurable number of hours (to be defined in system master settings). The following rules apply:</p><ul><li>If the transporter places a vehicle within the acceptance window, the DO is confirmed and removed from the queue.</li><li>If the transporter does not act within the acceptance window, the DO is automatically removed from their queue and reassigned to the next lowest-rate transporter.</li><li>This cascade continues until a transporter accepts or a manual intervention is required.</li><li>Expired or cascaded DOs are NOT visible to the original transporter — the queue shows only currently active, pending DOs.</li><li>Partial acceptance is not supported. A DO must be accepted or declined in full.</li></ul><h2>6.4 Vehicle Placement</h2><p>When accepting a DO, the transporter selects a vehicle from their Vehicle Master (only vehicles tagged to that transporter are available). This constitutes acceptance of the trip.</p><h2>6.5 Portal Operating Hours</h2><table><thead><tr><th><p><strong>Scenario</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Behaviour</strong></p></th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><p>DO created between 8:00 AM and 10:00 PM</p></td><td><p>DO is pushed to the transporter portal immediately upon creation in SAP.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>DO created after 10:00 PM</p></td><td><p>DO is held and pushed to the portal at 8:00 AM the following morning.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>DO created before 8:00 AM</p></td><td><p>DO is held and pushed to the portal at 8:00 AM the same morning.</p></td></tr></tbody></table><h2>6.6 Notifications</h2><p>When a new DO is assigned to a transporter, an alert must be triggered. Notification channel (SMS, email, in-app, or combination) to be confirmed during detailed design.</p><p><em>🔗 Integration: (1) DO details are pulled from SAP TMS when a new Delivery Order is created. (2) Once a transporter places a vehicle, the vehicle assignment must sync back to the SAP TMS Planning Screen automatically — no manual entry required on the SAP side.</em></p><h1>7. Create POD</h1><h2>7.1 Overview</h2><p>The Create POD page allows transporters to mark trips as completed by submitting a Proof of Delivery. Trips appear on this page once a freight invoice has been generated in SAP against the transporter. Upon POD submission, trip status is updated in SAP.</p><h2>7.2 Trip List View</h2><p>The page shows a list of all ongoing trips for the logged-in transporter (invoice generated, POD not yet submitted). Visible columns include: Product, Quantity, Destination, and Invoice Reference.</p><h2>7.3 POD Submission Form</h2><table><thead><tr><th><p><strong>#</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Field</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Input Type</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Notes</strong></p></th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><p>1</p></td><td><p>Date of Invoice</p></td><td><p>Auto (from SAP)</p></td><td><p>Pulled from the SAP freight invoice.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>2</p></td><td><p>Actual Date of Reporting</p></td><td><p>Manual</p></td><td><p>Date the vehicle actually reported at source.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>3</p></td><td><p>Date of Reaching Destination</p></td><td><p>Manual</p></td><td><p>Date the vehicle arrived at the delivery point.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>4</p></td><td><p>Date of Unloading at Destination</p></td><td><p>Manual</p></td><td><p>Date unloading was completed at destination.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>5</p></td><td><p>Detention at Source</p></td><td><p>Auto-calculated</p></td><td><p>Based on Invoice Date vs. Actual Reporting Date. Applied only if wait exceeds tolerance defined in master.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>6</p></td><td><p>Detention at Destination</p></td><td><p>Auto-calculated</p></td><td><p>Based on Date of Reaching Destination vs. Date of Unloading. Applied only if wait exceeds tolerance defined in master.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>7</p></td><td><p>Actual Qty Unloaded by Customer</p></td><td><p>Manual</p></td><td><p>Quantity confirmed by customer at destination.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>8</p></td><td><p>Shortages</p></td><td><p>Auto-calculated</p></td><td><p>Invoiced Qty minus Actual Qty Unloaded.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>9</p></td><td><p>LR Attachment (Lorry Receipt)</p></td><td><p>File Upload</p></td><td><p>Primary POD document. Multiple attachments may be supported — to be confirmed during design.</p></td></tr></tbody></table><h2>7.4 Detention Calculation Rules</h2><p>Detention at Source: Difference between the Invoice Date and Actual Date of Reporting. Detention is triggered only when this difference exceeds the tolerance threshold defined in the system master. The tolerance value (in hours) is configurable.</p><p>Detention at Destination: Difference between Date of Reaching Destination and Date of Unloading at Destination. Detention is triggered only when this difference exceeds the tolerance threshold. The tolerance value (in hours) is configurable.</p><p><em>🔗 Integration: Once a transporter submits the POD form, all details must sync back to SAP TMS and the trip status must be updated to POD Done. No manual update required on the SAP side.</em></p><h1>8. Invoice Management</h1><h2>8.1 Submit Invoice (Transporter View)</h2><h3>8.1.1 Trip Selection</h3><p>The transporter sees a list of all completed trips (POD submitted) for which an invoice has not yet been raised. The transporter can select one, multiple, or all eligible trips to club into a single invoice submission.</p><h3>8.1.2 Invoice Form</h3><table><thead><tr><th><p><strong>Field</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Input Type</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Notes</strong></p></th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><p>Invoice Number</p></td><td><p>Manual</p></td><td><p>Transporter's own invoice reference number.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Invoice Date</p></td><td><p>Manual</p></td><td><p>Date of the invoice as per transporter's records.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Invoice Amount (₹)</p></td><td><p>Manual</p></td><td><p>Total amount being claimed by the transporter.</p></td></tr></tbody></table><h3>8.1.3 Amount Validation</h3><p>The system automatically calculates the expected payable amount for the selected trips using the formula:</p><p><strong>Expected Amount  =  (Contract Rate × Qty)  +  Detention  −  Shortage Deductions</strong></p><p>If the transporter-entered invoice amount does not match the system-calculated expected amount, the discrepancy is flagged and visible to the reviewer during the approval stage. The reviewer has the authority to accept or reject the invoice based on the difference.</p><h3>8.1.4 Submission &amp; Status Tracking</h3><p>Once submitted, the invoice enters the approval workflow. The transporter can track the status of their invoice (Submitted / Under Review / Approved / Rejected) from this page.</p><p>If an invoice is rejected, it is returned to the transporter for correction and resubmission.</p><h2>8.2 Review Invoice (Internal User View)</h2><h3>8.2.1 Overview</h3><p>Internal users access a dedicated Review Invoice page (not visible to transporters). They can view all submitted invoices across transporters, apply transporter filters, and process invoices through the approval workflow.</p><h3>8.2.2 Approval Workflow</h3><table><thead><tr><th><p><strong>Level</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Role</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Action</strong></p></th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><p>L1</p></td><td><p>Checker</p></td><td><p>Reviews invoice details and validates against contract. Recommends approval or rejection.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>L2</p></td><td><p>Approver</p></td><td><p>Reviews L1 recommendation. Approves or rejects.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>L3</p></td><td><p>Final Approver</p></td><td><p>Final authority for approval. Approval at this level triggers SAP integration.</p></td></tr></tbody></table><h3>8.2.3 Rejection Flow</h3><p>At any level, if an invoice is rejected, the system notifies the transporter and returns the invoice to them for editing and resubmission. The reviewer must provide a reason for rejection.</p><p><em>🔗 Integration: Upon final approval (L3), the approved invoice details must automatically sync to SAP Finance to initiate the payment/vendor invoice process. No manual entry is required on the SAP side.</em></p><h1>9. Integration Summary</h1><p>All integrations are bidirectional or unidirectional as specified.
